TextFromImage helps you copy readable text from images on your device—without sending them to the cloud.

Open the floating panel from the toolbar icon: drop an image, paste from the clipboard (Ctrl+V), or pick a local file. Right-click any image on a web page and choose Extract text from image (TextFromImage). Optionally drag on the preview to crop a text block, then extract. Copy the result or download a .txt file.

What you get
• Local OCR with Tesseract (English + Spanish bundled—no language picker needed).
• Web images via context menu (optional per-site permission when required).
• Resizable image preview and result areas.
• UI in Spanish or English.

What it does not do
• No cloud OCR APIs—processing stays in your browser.
• No developer backend; images and extracted text are not uploaded to AI4Context servers.
• It does not translate text (planned as a separate extension).

Tips
• For book covers or posters, crop tightly to the text block before extracting.
• First OCR run may take a few seconds while language models load locally.
